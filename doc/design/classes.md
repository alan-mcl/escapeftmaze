## 1. Overview

Each class defines a character's **focus** (Combat, Stealth, or Magic), base **Hit Points**, **Action Points**, and **Magic Points**, level-up resource dice, which skills start **active**, and a cumulative **level ability progression** from class level 1 to 20.

All default classes share the **unity** experience table and grant **3 assignable skill points** per level. Actual HP/AP/MP at creation and on level-up are modified by race percentages and the **Brains** attribute (MP only).

Many classes grant access to one **magic colour** at a limited level (typically up to spell level 3 for combat/stealth hybrids, up to spell level 8 for dedicated magic classes). Spell access is expressed as *Colour Magic N* level abilities plus **spell picks** to learn individual spells from that book.

At character creation, players choose a **starting kit** for their class (unless their race supplies its own kit, as Dryad and Faerie do). Only kit bonuses matching the class focus apply.

|-------|---------|----------------------|--------------|

| Stealth | 9 | 15 | Ambush, skulduggery, ranged combat; several hybrid casters |

## 3. Combat classes

### Amazon

Raised by a fanatical sect of warrior women, the Amazon is a dauntless soldier versed in the ways of Purple magic. The Amazons primary interest is warfare and soldiering. She is also is at home in the wilderness, trained in tracking, survival and ambuscade.

Role: The Amazon is a versatile melee and ranged fighter. Her class abilities give her resilience against many debuffs and her access to the versatile Witchcraft school of magic give her a some non-weaponised options.

### Berserker

The Berserker's blessing and curse are the same - in the heat of battle the blood madness will come upon him or her, driving superhuman feats of strength and endurance. Sure there's a time and place for sniping and trading spells, but when things get down to an ugly brawl at tooth and claw range the Berserker is who you want in the mix.

### Hero

Dashing, charismatic and a natural leader.  The Hero leads the party from the front and is a master of melee combat. Although not the most intellectual of classes, the Hero does know about dungeons and loot. Role: Heroes belong in the front line, taking the hits and dealing out damage. With no special ranged abilities and no magical talent, he or she will need companions to step up in these other, less heroic situations.

### Samurai

In his home country, the Samurai was a member of the ruling military aristocracy. In the wilds of the Maze, he is a fearless warrior who combines a peerless mastery of swordplay with fierce sorcerous magic. The Samurai is an educated and literate man, with a broad background ranging from the arts to military strategy.

Role: A master of swordplay, the Samurai finds a place the the front row of the party. ### Priest

Role: The Priest has the strongest healing and White magic powers. Not especially interested in combat, he or she will need help with violent situations. A strict code of ethics means Priests work best in a party committed to honesty and fair dealings.

He is also perhaps the class most knowledgeable about the Maze itself, as his brotherhood has studied the subject extensively.

### Witch

The Witch has a store of wisdom on a wide range of subjects like astrology, herb lore, mythology, curses and the occult.  She is also the class most familiar with the ways of the faerie folk.

Role: As the strongest Purple magic user, the Witch is a potent specialist caster with both offensive and defensive options. She has special synergies with other Purple magic casters in the party.
